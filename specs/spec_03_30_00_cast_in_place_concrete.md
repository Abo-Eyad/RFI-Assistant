# SECTION 03 30 00 - CAST-IN-PLACE CONCRETE

## PART 1 - GENERAL
* **Scope**: This section specifies cast-in-place concrete, including formwork, reinforcement, concrete materials, mixture design, placement procedures, and finishes.

## PART 2 - PRODUCTS
### 2.1 Concrete Mixtures
* **Foundation Footings (C-1)**:
  * Minimum 28-day Compressive Strength: **4,000 psi**
  * Maximum Water-Cement Ratio: **0.45**
  * Air Content: **5% to 7%**
* **Columns and Shear Walls (C-2)**:
  * Minimum 28-day Compressive Strength: **5,000 psi**
  * Maximum Water-Cement Ratio: **0.40**
  * Air Content: **4% to 6%**
* **Slabs-on-Grade (C-3)**:
  * Minimum 28-day Compressive Strength: **3,500 psi**
  * Maximum Water-Cement Ratio: **0.50**
  * Air Content: **Non-air-entrained (interior slabs)**, **5% to 7% (exterior slabs)**
* **Portland Cement**: ASTM C150, Type I or Type II.

## PART 3 - EXECUTION
### 3.1 Cold Weather Concrete Placement
* **Temperature Limits**: When the ambient temperature falls below **40°F (4.4°C)** or is expected to fall below this temperature within 24 hours:
  * Do not place concrete on frozen ground or steel.
  * Maintain concrete temperature at placement between **55°F (13°C)** and **80°F (27°C)**.
  * Provide heating and protection to maintain concrete at a minimum of **50°F (10°C)** for at least **72 hours** after placement.

### 3.2 Hot Weather Concrete Placement
* **Temperature Limits**: When the ambient temperature is at or above **90°F (32.2°C)**:
  * Concrete temperature at the time of placement must not exceed **90°F (32.2°C)**.
  * Use chilled water or ice to control mixture temperature if necessary.
  * Cool forms, reinforcement, and subgrade before placing concrete.

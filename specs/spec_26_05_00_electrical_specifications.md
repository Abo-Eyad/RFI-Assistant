# SECTION 26 05 00 - COMMON WORK RESULTS FOR ELECTRICAL

## PART 1 - GENERAL
* **Scope**: This section specifies basic electrical materials and methods, including conduits, conductors, and boxes.

## PART 2 - PRODUCTS
### 2.1 Conduits
* **Rigid Metal Conduit (RMC)**: Galvanized steel, comply with UL 6.
  * Use in outdoor, exposed, or hazardous locations.
* **Electrical Metallic Tubing (EMT)**: Steel, comply with UL 797.
  * Use only in dry, indoor, concealed, or exposed locations where not subject to physical damage.
* **Liquidtight Flexible Metal Conduit (LFMC)**: Steel core with PVC jacket.
  * Use for connection to vibrating equipment (e.g., motors, pumps). Maximum length **6 feet (1.8 meters)**.

### 2.2 Conductors
* **Conductor Material**: Copper only. Aluminum conductors are not permitted.
* **Minimum Conductor Size**: **12 AWG** for power and lighting branch circuits.
* **Insulation Type**:
  * Dry and Concealed Indoor Locations: **THHN/THWN-2**, 600V rated.
  * Wet or Outdoor Locations: **THWN-2** or **XHHW-2**, 600V rated.

## PART 3 - EXECUTION
### 3.1 Conduit Installation
* **Support Spacing**:
  * Support conduits within **3 feet (900 mm)** of each outlet box, junction box, cabinet, or fitting.
  * Support intervals for EMT must not exceed **10 feet (3 meters)**.
* **Bends**: No more than the equivalent of **four quarter bends (360 degrees total)** in any conduit run between pull points.
* **Grounding**: Ensure electrical continuity of all metallic raceway systems. Install a dedicated green grounding conductor in all conduits.
